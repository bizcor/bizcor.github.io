---

I think the thing I prize most from you is my intelligence. There's no point in this letter in being falsely modest. I'm damned smart and I got it from you. I wonder from time to time what it must be like to be one of the 'beautiful people'. Generally when folks use that term, they mean physical beauty or great wealth. Although I have no problem with the way I look, I don't turn heads when I walk down the street. And while I'm financially comfortable, I'm not a person of substantial wealth. But I did win the IQ lottery. I have an incredibly useful tool to help get me through life. Not simply to survive it, but to really live it. I seek not to be better or worse than others, but to be content at the end of the day. Toward that goal I focus all of my intelligence.

I'm lucky that I'm also independent. I'm more than able to get along with people. And they leave an encounter with me feeling positive. But I follow the this personal rule: anyone can give me data--information or opinion--but I and I alone am allowed to make my conclusions. A friend once said to me, 'Nod your head and smile.
